Optimum viewing of the Leonid meteor shower in November 1998 was forecast for East Asia . Some predicted the shower to rival the greatest of the century in 1966 , others said it would be more modest . Meteor showers are hard to predict . Of particular concern in 1998 was the danger posed by the storms to earth-orbiting satellites . Some satellites were repositioned to be less likely targets , but authorities differed as to the real danger . Scientists studied the showers with various airborne instruments . In fact , the showers were more impressive viewed from Europe than East Asia and there was no damage to satellites .
