Two Libyans were indicted in 1991 for blowing up a Pan Am jumbo jet over Lockerbie , Scotland in 1988 . The United Nations imposed sanctions on Libya in 1992 because of their refusal to surrender the suspects . The sanctions included a ban on all international flights . After six years of sanctions , Libyan leader Moammar Gadhafi , faced with threats of additional sanctions , agreed in principle to hand over the suspects . The two suspects would be tried in the Netherlands by Scottish judges under Scottish law . Gadhafi wants guarantees , including a promise that the suspects would serve their sentences in the Netherlands or Libya if convicted .
