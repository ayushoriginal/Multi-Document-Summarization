The government 's antitrust case against Bill Gates ' Microsoft Corporation cited aggressive tactics in attempting to acquire Palm Pilot . The governments ' first salvo was to attack Gates ' contention that he was not involved by showing documents portraying his company obsessed with crushing its competitor . Leading prosecution witness , Netscape CEO James Barksdale , cited blatant threats by Microsoft , which Microsoft characterized as acceptable business practices . They also claimed that Netscape initiated some of the contacts . Despite being the target of the government 's case , Microsoft earnings continued to grow . In its defense , Microsoft said that the `` New Economy '' was also on trial and that new antitrust definitions were needed .
