South Africa 's Truth and Reconciliation Commission was established to clear the air after the ugly apartheid struggle . Retired Archbishop Tutu headed the Commission . It worked two plus years , holding countrywide meetings . It was controversial from the start . Commission leaks prompted de Klerk , the last white President , to threaten a court challenge The final 3,500-page report was issued on 29 October 1998 . The white majority received the greater blame for rights violations , but the black ANC was also cited . De Klerk 's name did not appear in the report . Botha , the Inkatha Party , the ruling ANC , and Winnie Mandela were specifically cited .
