Swiss Air flight 111 crashed off Nova Scotia on Sept. 2 , 1998 killing all 129 passengers and crew . The cause has not been determined , but there was extreme heat damage to the front of the aircraft and it is suspected that an in-flight entertainment system had electrical problems . Swiss Air , while insisting that the system was installed correctly , has disconnected the system in other aircraft as a precaution . The airline immediately offered $ 20,000 to families of the victims and , having opted out of a $ 75,000 liability cap , began work on a plan to provide compensation of approximately $ 137,000 per family .
