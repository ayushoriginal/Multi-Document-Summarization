The United Nations Security Council established a War Crimes Tribunal in 1993 to investigate and prosecute crimes arising from the breakup of Yugoslavia . In October 1998 , the tribunal secured its third conviction when a Bosnian Serb plead guilty to 13 murders . Also that month , the trial of four for 1992 atrocities in a Bosnian prison camp was nearing completion . Also , Kosovo became an area of contention . The Yugoslavian government was claiming the tribunal had no jurisdiction over the 9-month rebellion and was refusing investigators visas . The U.N. tribunal sought to enter the province and investigate atrocities reported by both sides .
