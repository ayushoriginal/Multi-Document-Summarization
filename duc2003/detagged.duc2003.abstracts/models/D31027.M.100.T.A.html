In Iran prior to the 1998 election of the 86-member Assembly of Experts , the Council of Guardians , dominated by political hard-liners , rejected 214 of 400 prospective candidates , disqualifying many supporters of the popular moderate reformist President Mohammed Khatami who criticized the selection process . Many moderates pledged to boycott the election . On election day official media reported an `` unprecedented and unique '' turnout , but few voters were seen at the polls . The conservatives won a decisive victory as expected with less than half of the eligible voters going to the polls . Hard-liners won at least 54 of the 86-seats and the moderates only 13 .
