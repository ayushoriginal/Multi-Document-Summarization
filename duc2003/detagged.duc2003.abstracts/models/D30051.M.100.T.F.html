Violence continues in Yugoslavia as the International Court in the Hague pushes to have inspectors return to investigate instances of war crimes . President Milosevic continues to deny war crimes even amid reports of a massacre , described by a survivor , and murders by Serbian police in Kosovo . The General Assembly voted to add three judges to the war crimes tribunal , which scored its first guilty verdicts , one of a Serb who confessed to killing fourteen . The war crimes tribunal branded Yugoslavia a `` rogue state '' for denying entry to inspectors but , later , an agreement was reached to allow inspectors to return in November .
