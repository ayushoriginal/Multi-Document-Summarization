The IMF and the US Treasury Department are preparing a loan package of $ 30 billion or more to stabilize Brazil 's economy as Latin American economic officials decry paltry IMF programs to their countries . Brazil is suffering from a failing economy carrying both a large trade and a budget deficit . The re-election of Brazil 's President by a narrow margin dims hopes for economic reforms . He will have to initiate rapid and unpopular steps to save his country 's economy . Influencing the IMF bailout decision is the slow progress Brazil has shown on reforms that would cut government expenses over the long term .
