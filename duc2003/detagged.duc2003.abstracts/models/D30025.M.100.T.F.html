Brazil 's economic woes dominated the political scene as President Cardoso faced re-election . His economic program had led to a reduction of the country 's high inflation rate to near zero . However , trade and budget deficits and the need for tax and budget reforms to curb spending kept the economic picture in serious straits . Cardoso easily won re-election as confidence in his program remained high . The victories of his political rivals in gubernatorial and legislative races left further stern measures in doubt . The nation hoped for a relief package from the International Monetary Fund .
