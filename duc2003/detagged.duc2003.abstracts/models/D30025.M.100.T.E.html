Brazilian President Fernando Henrique Cardoso was re-elected in the first round of the election with 56 percent of the vote , eliminating the need for a runoff . Cardoso easily defeated three-time presidential candidate Luiz Inacio Lula de Silva , who garnered only about 34 percent of the vote . The election , held as Brazil was in the midst of an economic crisis , ensured that continuity of leadership would be maintained . Newly re-elected President Cardoso delayed announcing plans for economic reform until after the gubernatorial elections . Those elections , which saw many rivals elected , cast doubt on the future of Cardoso 's economic reforms .
