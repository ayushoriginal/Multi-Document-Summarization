The fate of Asia 's oldest airline , PAL , is uncertain . Negotiations with Cathay Pacific Airways to infuse $ 100 million dollars into the company collapsed when PAL Chairman Tan refused to agree to major job cuts and to relinquish management control to Cathay . PAL is buried under a $ 2.2 billion dollar debt it cannot repay and $ 1 million a day losses . A replacement investor is unlikely and President Estrada , though supported by Tan in the election , has rejected a government bailout . PAL 's financial troubles were exacerbated by a two-week shutdown in September due to a dispute with its largest union .
