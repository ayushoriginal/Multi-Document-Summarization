In 1998 two Libyans indicted in 1991 for the Lockerbie bombing were still in Libya . In 1992 the U . N. voted sanctions against Libya for its refusal to turn over the suspects . Suffering from the sanctions , Gadhafi alternated between defiance and acceptance . In August of 1998 the U.N. proposed a trial in the Netherlands under Scottish law which Libya accepted in principle , but insisted that any sentence be served in the Netherlands or Libya . The U.N. threatened a tightening of sanctions . Then , in late November , the U . N. Secretary General hinted that he might broker an agreement . Optimism arose , but the issue was still in doubt .
