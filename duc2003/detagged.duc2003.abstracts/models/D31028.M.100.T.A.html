For four days in September 1998 Hurricane Georges plowed through the Caribbean affecting more than 30 million people . A high pressure system kept the storm on its east-to-west course . More than 500 were killed , more than 138,000 left homeless and property damage exceeded $ 5 billion . Authorities in Haiti and the Dominican Republic seemed unprepared as reflected in death rates of 221 and more than 280 respectively . Widespread destruction of homes in Haiti and Puerto Rico presented major problems of funding because of the number of uninsured homes and the lack of local financial resources , but U.S. and international assistance was quickly arranged .
