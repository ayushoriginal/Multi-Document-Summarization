The South African Truth and Reconciliation Commission , chaired by Archbishop Desmond Tutu , completed a lengthy study of human rights violations , including torture and murder , in that country , 1960-1994 . The apartheid government was the most culpable but the African National Congress also had a share of the blame . The `` truth '' part of the report was the easiest : it listed crimes and named names , including former president Botha , Inkatha leader Buthelezi , and even Winnie Mandela . Information implicating former president de Klerk was withheld . Reconciliation promised to be more difficult . Amnesty was proposed for those who came forward ( perhaps within a given period ) , confessed , and requested amnesty .
