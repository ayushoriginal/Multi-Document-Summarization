Invoking the Sherman Act , the Justice Department and 20 states have begun anti-trust proceedings against Microsoft Corporation . Key to the government 's case is the allegation , supported by Netscape , that Microsoft suggested in a June 1995 meeting that Netscape join Microsoft in dividing the internet software market . In a taped deposition , Microsoft Chairman Bill Gates denied any knowledge of such an offer . Microsoft lawyers later produced an e-mail from Netscape President James Barkdale which suggests Netscape was a willing participant in the internet software discussion . Despite the on-going suit , Microsoft made an unsuccessful bid to acquire the software business behind the Palm Pilot .
